How the RV came about

The term "virtual reality" was invented in the 1980s, but the vision of a world Simulated immersion has been a part of science fiction for decades. (Steven Spielberg's recent film Ready Player One offers a modern interpretation.) The military carried out some of the first experiments in the 1960s and 1970s. That's when engineer Tom Furness, looking for ways to help fighter pilots mastering the complicated controls of aircraft, he began to work on what eventually became would turn into a surprisingly realistic airplane simulator: the Super Cockpit. Boasted of images Computer-generated 3D projected inside the pilot's helmet, including with sound effects. "From the beginning I knew how powerful it could be," says Furness, who has been recognized as a pioneer of the RV and is currently 75 years old.
